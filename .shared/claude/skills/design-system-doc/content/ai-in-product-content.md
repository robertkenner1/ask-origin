## Usage

Choosing when to use generative AI is as important as how you use it. It allows us to create dynamic, unique content that no other technology can. But it can also be slow, expensive, and unpredictable.

### When to use generative AI

Grammarly is an AI writing partner, and generative AI content can make that persona feel more intelligent and personal. Use generative AI content when:

* A personalized element can make the product feel warmer and more aware
* Adding unique details makes an action more straightforward or urgent
* A bespoke analysis would provide better assistance

### When not to use it

Grammarly is also a software product with technical requirements and limitations. Avoid generative AI content when:

* Specific information is available in simpler ways. It may be easier and more reliable to reference a database than to use generative AI.
* Product speed is essential. Generative AI is getting faster, but it still introduces delays into the product.
* Gains are marginal. While generative AI content can be used almost anywhere, its cost and complexity mean it should be reserved for high-impact implementations.

## Unique considerations

### Follow Content Design principles

To our users, AI-generated text is no different than any other kind. So, if it appears in the interfaces of our products, we hold it to the same standards as all product content. To see how we ensure high-quality content at Grammarly, start with our [best practices](/content/best-practices) and [voice and tone guidelines](/content/voice-and-tone).

If you have questions or need Content Design assistance, reach out in #ama-content-design on Slack.

### Pronouns

In general, content should follow our standard guidance to use third-person pronouns when speaking as the product or company.

However, we also allow users to chat with the AI and the models we use default to first-person pronouns for their responses. Changing this is difficult and unreliable, and the results can sound awkward.

In these cases, the product should use first-person pronouns.

**User prompt:** â€œDo you like my essay?â€

Content in the product should clarify that the chat persona is a specific agent within the larger Superhuman product. Use phrases like â€œChat with our AIâ€ to mitigate this inconsistency.

### Tense

If we use generative AI content to describe an action the user can take, the model may default to past tense because it has already generated a potential response. The user, however, still needs to take action, so the text should be in the present tense.

### Text length

While AI models can follow general guidance about text length, they cannot reliably follow a strict character count. You should allow some flexibility when designing UI elements that include generative AI content.

### Default text

Generative AI models sometimes fail or generate unusable text. To mitigate these issues, create default text with a generic version of what the model would generate that can be used when the issues occur.

## How to work with generative AI in product content

If we want generative AI content to meet these standards, we need ways to guide the models in doing so. Apply this process and work with technical partners in linguistics and engineering to generate high-quality product content.

### Define rules

Write a list of rules the generative AI content should follow. These should include all relevant elements of the content, such as formatting, phrasing, and length.

Review and revise these rules with your technical partners. While some rules can be included in the generative AI prompt, others may be implemented in pre- or post-processing steps that modify the content. Discuss whatâ€™s possible and find a solution that meets your needs.

### Write samples

Often, the best way to get generative AI to produce reliable output is to provide samples of what you want. Content Designers should write several samples that cover the range of desired variations in output. More diverse samples will result in more varied output. Four or five samples is often enough, but more can be added if needed.

Samples can be written from scratch, but if the model already exists, you can use its outputs as a starting point. Revise a diverse range of outputs into versions that follow your rules. You can also write new samples to expand the range of outputs.

### Review and adjust output

When the model is ready to generate content, review a broad range of its outputs. Document where and how they donâ€™t meet the guidelines. Discuss the issues with your technical partners and figure out how to address them. You may have to adjust your rules, provide new or revised samples, make changes to processing, or decide to accept the imperfections.

Then, repeat this step as needed until the results are ready to ship.
