# Numbers

**Numbers** mean the symbols and words that represent measurements, counts, and values. This section is organized alphabetically and applies to content in product UI.

For a full breakdown of Grammarly‚Äôs approach to grammar and usage in other external-facing content, visit the <Link href="https://coda.io/d/_dNw1zZnMEND/Grammarly-Style-Guide_suVxisx4">Grammarly Company Style Guide</Link>.

## But first, a note about localization

We write for the Grammarly product using American English spelling, grammar, and formatting for text and numbers. Although a user‚Äôs writing _is_ corrected by Grammarly based on the dialect of English they choose, the rest of the UI is not localized into either English dialects or other languages. <Link href="https://www.w3.org/International/questions/qa-i18n">Learn about localization and internationalization</Link>.

Despite this, it‚Äôs important to consider how machine or AI translations by third-party apps and browser extensions could affect our product. For example, certain browser extensions can translate text in UI from English into a different language.

To compensate for this and maximize comprehension:

- Use consistent terminology when applicable
- Write in American English with American formatting
- Apply standards from GDS and the [Company Brand Style Guide](https://coda.io/d/Grammarly-Style-Guide_dNw1zZnMEND/Grammarlys-Plans_su4UNXs7)

Keep global political perspectives front of mind when writing for the product. Grammarly‚Äôs audience is, in part, people who learn English as a second language. Look for what could be misunderstood and edit it for complete clarity.

## Currency

Above all, be crystal clear about the monetary value you are describing, especially when relaying to a user how much something costs or how much they will spend. Consider, for example, that the *$* symbol can refer to US dollars, Australian dollars, Canadian dollars, Mexican pesos, and a few other currencies, too.

### Decimals

Always include decimals to the hundredth place. Even if a monetary value is whole (without cents), use ‚Äú.00‚Äù at the end for precision and consistency in formatting. Include decimals for zero values as well.

Do not shorten a decimal to the tenth place for any reason. Likewise, do not give values with fractions of cents.

#### Exceptions 

- **For whole values in a heading (h1, h2, h3, etc.):** It‚Äôs OK to omit decimals to save space. One example of this is the <Link href="https://www.grammarly.com/plans">plan comparison page</Link>, where prices are listed as h3 headings without decimals because they‚Äôre all whole values. If any of the three had cents, then all values should use decimals for consistency.
- **Some currencies do not have cents**, such as the Japanese Yen, while others include cents to the thousandth place, such as the Kuwaiti Dinar.  While Grammarly does not accept payments in these currencies today, be sure to follow the local standards by currency and location to avoid unfamiliar formats.

#### Comma for thousands, period for decimals

In most English-speaking countries, commas mark the thousands place of whole numbers. Periods delineate the whole numbers from fractions of currency (cents). The symbol comes before the value without a space in between.

Use this separator format when displaying currency in the United States, Mexico, United Kingdom, Ireland, Liechtenstein, Malta, and Canada when writing in English.

### Significant figures

When deciding how to use decimals, ask yourself:
- Is your statistic absolutely exact or is it more like an educated guess? 
- How much precision does the user need to make an informed choice? 

To avoid the impression that a rough estimate is an unimpeachable fact, consider rounding up or down to the nearest whole number for inexact stats and percentages.

If your use case warrants precise metrics, write out statistics and percentages to a maximum of 2 decimal places. Display more precise data only when absolutely necessary to convince or inform.

## Times

Use numerals and include the hour and minute digits.

Abbreviate AM and PM in all caps, without periods. Make sure there is a space between the hour/minute digits and the AM and PM abbreviations.

For noon and midnight, spelling it out more clearly (12 noon; 12 midnight) is preferred in places like body text or when there is more space. But if we must follow the # AM/# PM format, style as such: 12 PM (noon) and 12 AM (midnight).
